Clay Pot

Clay pots are porous, alkaline, eco-friendly containers made from natural clay, offering health benefits like mineral infusion and pH balance, while also enhancing food flavor and requiring slow cooking with less oil.
They can be used for cooking or water storage, with different types ranging from unglazed cooking pots that require soaking to decorative garden pots.

Plastic Pot

Plastic pots are lightweight, durable, and versatile containers for growing plants, available in many sizes and styles like nursery, decorative, self-watering, and hanging options. Key benefits include portability,
affordability, and good moisture retention, though dark pots can get too hot in direct sun, and it's important to choose pots with drainage holes and, for outdoor use, UV-resistant options.

Metal Pot

Metal pots can be either cookware or planters, with both having distinct properties and uses. Cookware is made from heat-conductive but non-reactive metals like stainless steel or clad copper, while garden pots are
often made from lightweight and durable metals with finishes to prevent rust and overheating.

Porcelain

A porcelain pot is a type of durable ceramic ware made from a refined clay body, typically including kaolin, fired at high temperatures (\(1,200-1,450^{\circ }C\)). This process, known as vitrification, results in a hard, dense, and often translucent material that is less porous and more resistant to water and chipping than earthenware or stoneware.
